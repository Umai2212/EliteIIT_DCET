About the Exam

What is DCET and Who Should Appear?

DCET (Diploma Common Entrance Test) is conducted by KEA for diploma holders in Engineering or Architecture who want direct admission to the 2nd year (lateral entry) of B.E./B.Tech or B.Arch programmes in Karnataka colleges. If you have a diploma and want to enter engineering degree directly at 2nd year — DCET is the exam.

DCET is not the same as KCET. It is a separate exam designed exclusively for diploma holders — students who have completed a 3-year diploma in Engineering or Architecture from a government or private polytechnic and want to step into a degree programme without repeating from Year 1.

The exam is conducted annually by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA). Scores are used for admission to government, aided, and unaided engineering colleges across Karnataka under the lateral entry scheme.

Competition is real — tens of thousands of diploma holders appear statewide. A structured long-term preparation programme is the difference between a seat in a top college and a missed opportunity.

What is DCET and who is eligible?

DCET (Diploma Common Entrance Test) is conducted by KEA for students who have completed a 3-year diploma in Engineering or Architecture and want to join 2nd year B.E./B.Tech or B.Arch programmes in Karnataka colleges through the lateral entry scheme. You must have completed your diploma from a recognised polytechnic.

Does DCET have negative marking?

No negative marking in DCET. Each correct answer carries 1 mark. No marks are deducted for wrong answers. This makes attempt strategy different from exams like JEE — you should attempt every question.

What is the DCET exam pattern?

DCET is a 100-mark, 90-minute offline exam. For Engineering stream, the paper has three sections — Mathematics, Basic Sciences (Physics/Chemistry), and branch-specific subjects. For Architecture stream, sections include Mathematics, Drawing, and Spatial Aptitude. The paper is multiple choice with no negative marking.

All 4 Elite IIT branches in Bangalore offer DCET coaching — Jayanagar, Koramangala, Malleshwaram, and Yelahanka. All branches run the same programme with the same batch size limit of under 20 students.

Is DCET coaching different from KCET coaching?

Yes — completely different. KCET is for PUC students and tests Class 11 and 12 syllabus. DCET is for diploma holders and tests diploma-level Engineering or Architecture subjects. The syllabus, exam pattern, and difficulty level are all different. KCET coaching material is not useful for DCET — Elite IIT builds DCET coaching from the correct source content.

How long should I prepare for DCET?

A 6 to 8-month long-term programme is the most effective for DCET. This gives enough time to cover all sections systematically, complete multiple mock test cycles, and revise weak areas before the exam. Students who join early consistently outperform those who start 2–3 months before the exam.
